# References & Sources for Migraine Forecast v2

## Clinical Trials
1. **NCT07301008**: *Rimegepant as Preemptive Treatment for Predictable Trigger-Induced Migraine in Adult Patients in the US*  
   URL: [https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07301008](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07301008)  
   **Relevance:** Validates the clinical approach of preemptive treatment when predictable triggers (exercise, alcohol, fasting, air travel) are anticipated.
2. **NCT05754190**: *Assessing Symptom and Mood Dynamics in Pain Using the Smartphone Application SOMA*  
   URL: [https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT05754190](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT05754190)  
   **Relevance:** Demonstrates modern approaches to using smartphone-based Ecological Momentary Assessment (EMA) to identify computational predictors of pain transitions.
3. **NCT07567300**: *Common Pain Mechanisms in Migraine, Migraine-Related Neck Pain, and Lower Back Pain*  
   URL: [https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07567300](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07567300)  
   **Relevance:** Provides evidence on central sensitization and how shared neuroanatomical pathways (trigemino-cervical complex) result in concurrent spinal pain.
4. **NCT02910921**: *Individualized Prediction of Migraine Attacks Using a Mobile Phone App and Fitbit*  
   URL: [https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT02910921](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT02910921)  
   **Relevance:** Tests the feasibility of using mobile phones and Fitbit wearables (HRV and physiologic data) to accurately predict migraine attacks.
5. **NCT05454319**: *Cerebri Biofeedback Feasibility Trial*  
   URL: [https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT05454319](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT05454319)  
   **Relevance:** Demonstrates the therapeutic approach of providing on-screen HRV biofeedback to teach patients to increase parasympathetic tone, reducing migraine frequency.

## Peer-Reviewed Literature & Preprints
1. **Weather, air pollution, and migraine: A case-time series analysis examining environmental exposures and transient health outcomes recorded via smartphone application.**  
   *PubMed ID:* 42109518  
   *URL:* [https://europepmc.org/article/MED/42109518](https://europepmc.org/article/MED/42109518)  
   **Relevance:** Massive app-based dataset linking drops in barometric pressure, cold winter temps, and air pollution to migraine onsets.  
   *(A copy of the PDF has been downloaded to `docs/superpowers/references/PMID_42109518.pdf`)*
2. **Migraine generator network and spreading depression dynamics as neuromodulation targets in episodic migraine**  
   *arXiv ID:* 1303.2256  
   *URL:* [https://arxiv.org/abs/1303.2256](https://arxiv.org/abs/1303.2256)  
   **Relevance:** Models the migraine generator network as a dynamical system susceptible to spatio-temporal noxious inputs and cortical spreading depression.  
   *(A copy of the PDF has been downloaded to `docs/superpowers/references/arxiv_1303_2256.pdf`)*
3. **Understanding migraine using dynamical network biomarkers**  
   *arXiv ID:* 1404.6126  
   *URL:* [https://arxiv.org/abs/1404.6126](https://arxiv.org/abs/1404.6126)  
   **Relevance:** Proposes using dynamical network biomarkers to identify when the migraine generator subnetwork hits a critical tipping point.  
   *(A copy of the PDF has been downloaded to `docs/superpowers/references/arxiv_1404_6126.pdf`)*
4. **Migraine Triggers and Oxidative Stress: A Narrative Review and Synthesis**  
   *Author:* Jonathan M. Borkum  
   *DOI:* 10.1111/head.12725  
   *URL:* [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26639834](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26639834)  
   **Relevance:** A unifying synthesis demonstrating that disparate migraine triggers (alcohol, sleep loss, weather) all generate oxidative stress, activating TRPA1 cation channels. *(Note: This paper is closed access and thus the original PDF could not be automatically downloaded).*
5. **Wearable Nocturnal Autonomic and Sleep Biomarkers for Predicting Next-Day Headache and Identifying Nociplastic Pain in Patients with Migraine.**  
   *PMCID:* PMC13206900  
   *URL:* [https://europepmc.org/article/PMC/13206900](https://europepmc.org/article/PMC/13206900)  
   **Relevance:** Found that nocturnal autonomic measures like phasic EDA and HRV, gathered via wearables, are physiological indicators related to next-day headache.  
   *(A copy of the PDF has been downloaded to `docs/superpowers/references/PMC13206900.pdf`)*
6. **Convergent Sensing: Integrating Biometric and Environmental Monitoring in Next-Generation Wearables.**  
   *PMCID:* PMC12838745  
   *URL:* [https://europepmc.org/article/PMC/12838745](https://europepmc.org/article/PMC/12838745)  
   **Relevance:** Makes the case for fusing data from environmental sensors (air quality, ambient temperature, atmospheric pressure) with physiological sensors (EDA, HRV) to contextualize bodily stress.  
   *(A copy of the PDF has been downloaded to `docs/superpowers/references/PMC12838745.pdf`)*
7. **Vagally Mediated Heart Rate Variability and Attachment-Related Avoidance in Pediatric Migraine: A Mediated Pathway to Somatic Symptoms Severity.**  
   *PMCID:* PMC12731400  
   *URL:* [https://europepmc.org/article/PMC/12731400](https://europepmc.org/article/PMC/12731400)  
   **Relevance:** Demonstrates that psychological stressors can result in lower resting vagally-mediated HRV, which then acts as a pathway to increased somatic and pain symptoms.  
   *(A copy of the PDF has been downloaded to `docs/superpowers/references/PMC12731400.pdf`)*
8. **Project Hermes: A Model-Agnostic Validation Layer for Wearable Health Prediction Systems**  
   *arXiv ID:* 2602.18643  
   *URL:* [https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.18643](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.18643)  
   **Relevance:** Introduces a model-agnostic validation layer that treats signal confirmation as a sequential decision problem. By eliciting targeted user feedback downstream of arbitrary predictors, it achieved a 34% reduction in false positive rate in a longitudinal case study of migraine prediction.  
   *(A copy of the PDF has been downloaded to `docs/superpowers/references/arxiv_2602_18643.pdf`)*
